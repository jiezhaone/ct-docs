# Activity Content & Completion Standards

## About this Journal

To deepen the practice of the "Three Links of Cultivation," we issue a "Merits and Wisdom Learning Journal." Its purpose is to guide and encourage students to diligently cultivate the three areas: Scripture Study, Meritorious Service, and Meditation. Through this balanced practice, students can nurture virtuous roots and merits, and together move toward the great path of awakening.

The journal can be set as a one-year goal from the day you receive it.

Each section entails practical activities. Record your progress on a regular basis and have your effort certified by the Dharma Masters at your meditation center. Awards will be given for exemplary completion of the activities.

::: tip
There are two levels of completion: **Basic** and **Advanced**.
:::

## Completion Standards at a Glance

| Activity | Basic | Advanced |
|----------|-------|----------|
| Take Courses | 2 terms of meditation classes or the Mahayana Sutra Study Class | Same as Basic |
| Recite Sutras | 108 times | 365 times |
| Recite Dharma Excerpts | 40 excerpts | 40 excerpts |
| Study Dharma Lectures | 24 Q&A sets (*Effortless Composure in the Changing World*) | 24 Q&A sets + one of three e-books |
| Vow-making | 108 entries | 300 entries |
| Self-reflection | 108 entries | 300 entries |
| Meritorious Service | 60 hours | 120 hours |
| Meditation | 108 sessions (≥ 12 at center) | 200 sessions (≥ 36 at center) |

## Basic

### I. Scripture Study

*To walk the path of enlightenment by studying the teachings*

In this section, students can take courses, recite sutras individually or collectively, and study the Grand Master's Dharma lectures and Dharma excerpts to:

- gain a deeper understanding of the teachings,
- solidify right views in cultivation, and
- integrate insights into daily life.

#### 1. Take Courses

You can meet this goal in one of the following ways:

- complete two terms of meditation classes;
- complete two terms of the Mahayana Sutra Study Class; or
- keep attending classes steadily by following the meditation center's course schedule.

#### 2. Recite Sutras Individually or Collectively

Recite sutras individually or collectively as your daily practice. You may select a sutra or a repentance text, or adapt them according to Chung Tai and your meditation center's Dharma events.

**Goal:** Complete 108 times of sutra recitation. *(For sutras divided into volumes, each volume counts as one unit. For sutras not divided into volumes, the complete recitation of one sutra counts as one unit.)*

#### 3. Recite Dharma Excerpts

Select a Dharma excerpt by the Grand Master, and recite it multiple times; then internalize the wisdom of the verse in daily life.

**Goal:** Complete 40 Dharma excerpts.

#### 4. Study Dharma Lectures

Study the Grand Master's Dharma lectures on a regular basis, taking the words into careful consideration; then contemplate how to integrate them with daily practice.

**Goal:** Complete the review of 24 sets of questions and answers from the English e-book *Effortless Composure in the Changing World*.

### II. Meritorious Service

*To develop mindfulness by cultivating merits with a pure mind*

In this section, students are encouraged to proactively engage in meritorious service through vow-making and self-reflection to purify body, speech and mind. As a result, students will cultivate blessings and merits, and deepen their practice.

#### 1. Vow-making

Students may choose to make vows:

- The Four Great Vows (3 consecutive times per entry)
- The Ten Great Vows of Samantabhadra Bodhisattva (1 time per entry)

**Goal:** Complete 108 entries of vow-making.

#### 2. Self-reflection

Examine whether your body, speech and mind follow the words of a Dharma excerpt. (1 instance per entry)

**Goal:** Complete 108 entries of self-reflection.

#### 3. Meritorious Service

Students can volunteer at their meditation center or Chung Tai to help others while generating merits.

**Goal:** Accumulate a total of 60 hours of meritorious service at Chung Tai or a meditation center.

### III. Meditation

*To realize the intrinsic nature by abiding in the present moment*

The activities in this section help students foster a daily meditation practice and participate in retreats organized by the meditation center or Chung Tai, thereby cultivating a mind of stillness, purity and clarity.

- Every 30-minute sitting meditation counts as one session. For example, 60 minutes of meditation equals two sessions.
- Participation in a retreat organized by the meditation center or Chung Tai can be counted based on the number of sessions attended (includes half-day / one-day retreats, or personal meditation practice).

**Goal:** Accumulate a total of 108 sitting meditation sessions, of which at least 12 sessions must be completed during retreats at Chung Tai or a meditation center.
